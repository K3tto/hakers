SOCIAL ISSUE:

TRAFFICKING - Traffickers transport victims to 'cybersex dens' and use webcams to stream sexual assaults in real time through a computer to the internet for live distant purchasers across the world.

ONLINE HARASSMENT - Online harassment can be defined as the use of information and communication technologies by an individual or group to repeatedly cause harm to another person.

CYBER BULLYING - Cyberbullying is bullying with the use of digital technologies. It can take place on social media, messaging platforms, gaming platforms and mobile phones.

ACCOUNT HACKING - Account hijacking is a type of cyberattack during which a hacker takes control of the victim's account associated with a computer device or online service.

CYBER-STALKING - involves following a person's movements across the Internet by posting messages (sometimes threatening).

CREDIT CARD FRAUD - Credit card fraud is a form of identity theft that involves an unauthorized taking of another's credit card information for the purpose of charging purchases to the account or removing funds from it.







PROFESSIONAL ISSUE:

LOST REVENUE IN BUSINESS - - One of the worst outcomes of a cyberattack is a sudden drop in revenue, as cautious customers move elsewhere to protect themselves against cybercrime.

REPUTATIONAL DAMAGE ON BUSINESSES - Reputational damage can result in loss of revenue, loss of business partners, employee turnover, and loss of confidence from the board and stakeholders responsible for your corporate governance.

DATA BREACH IN BUSINESS - - A cybercrime data breach at your small businesses can have far reaching consequences including fines, loss of consumer trust. a data breach exposes confidential, sensitive, or protected information to an unauthorized person.

NATIONAL SECURITY CONCERN - For national security concerns, the likely cybercrime threats deal on espionage activity, disruption, sabotage, politically motivated attacks and those which involve terrorism activities.
